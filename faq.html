Getting Started

Find the right starting point and learn how to build confidence step by step.

Begin with the Computer Basics lessons, then move step by step into Microsoft Word and Microsoft Excel.

The beginner pathway is designed for people who may have little or no previous computer experience. Start with the introductory lessons on understanding the computer, desktop icons, files and folders, and essential computer skills. After that, continue with the Microsoft Word and Excel beginner series.

Do not rush. Watch one lesson, practise what you learn, complete the available assignment, and then move to the next lesson. Progress becomes easier when you learn consistently—one clear step at a time.

Start With Computer Basics →

No. You do not need previous computer experience to begin learning with Athanas Inspires.

The lessons are created especially for absolute beginners and explain each step in simple, practical language. You can start from the basics, practise at your own pace, and build confidence gradually.

It is completely normal to feel unsure at the beginning. You do not need to know everything before you start—you only need the willingness to learn, practise, and keep moving forward.

Start Learning From the Basics →

You can begin learning with a smartphone, but a computer is strongly recommended for practical ICT training.

A smartphone allows you to watch lessons, read guides, join the WhatsApp ICT community, use some website resources, and understand important concepts. However, lessons involving file management, Microsoft Word, Microsoft Excel, keyboard shortcuts, and other computer skills are best practised on a desktop or laptop.

You do not have to wait until you own a computer before you start learning. Begin with what you have, then practise on a computer whenever one becomes available. Watching helps you understand—but practising is what builds real confidence and skill.

Explore Beginner ICT Lessons →

Follow the lessons from the basics and move forward one step at a time.

Start with the Computer Basics series, where you learn about the computer, desktop icons, files and folders, and essential everyday computer skills. After that, continue with the Microsoft Word series, then move into the Microsoft Excel series.

Within each series, begin with Session 1 and continue in order. Avoid skipping lessons, because each session builds on skills introduced earlier. For the best results, watch the lesson, practise the steps yourself, complete the available assignment, and only then continue to the next session.

Learning in order helps you build a strong foundation and move steadily from confusion to clarity.

View the Recommended Learning Path →

Practise the skills yourself as soon as possible after watching the lesson.

Repeat the steps on your own computer, complete the available assignment, and try using the skill in a simple real-life task. For example, after a Microsoft Word lesson, create a short document. After an Excel lesson, build a small table and practise the formulas you learned.

Do not worry if you make mistakes. Mistakes are part of learning and often help you remember the correct steps. You can replay the lesson, pause where necessary, ask for help through the WhatsApp ICT community, and continue practising until the skill feels familiar.

Watching introduces the skill—but practice turns it into confidence.

Pause, identify the exact step that is confusing you, and try it again slowly.

Replay the relevant part of the lesson, pause after each step, and compare what appears on your screen with what is shown in the video. Check simple details such as spelling, cell references, formula symbols, file locations, or whether you clicked the correct option.

When the problem continues, take a clear screenshot and explain what you were trying to do, what happened, and what you expected to happen. You can then ask for help through the Athanas Inspires WhatsApp ICT community.

Getting stuck does not mean you cannot learn. It simply shows you have reached a point that needs more attention. Stay patient, ask questions, and keep moving forward.

Open the Assignments page, find the correct course and session, then click the “Download Assignment” button.

Check the filename and session number before opening it to make sure you downloaded the correct assignment. When the resource is provided as a ZIP file, extract it first before trying to use the files inside.
